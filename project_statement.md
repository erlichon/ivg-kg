## 1. Problem

Large language models produce fluent, confident answers, but a reader cannot tell *how* any given statement was arrived at. Three failure-relevant cases are conflated in the output: facts the model **retrieved** from reliable structured knowledge, facts it obtained by **reasoning** over several pieces of structured knowledge, and facts it **fabricated**. (What a tool can measure post-hoc is *supportability* — whether the evidence needed to ground a claim exists in the graph — so from §3 onward the middle category is named **reasoned-supportable**.) Knowledge graphs (KGs) store high-quality factual triples about entities; *multimodal* KGs additionally attach images and textual descriptions to each entity, making them a rich substrate for multimedia analytics. A text-rich **books** slice anchors the textual-content modality; an **image slice** — primarily **visual artworks** (paintings), with biological-taxa range-maps as a verified fallback — instantiates the image modality, where the image carries facts the triples do not. The study examines each evidence modality **within its slice**, with an unconfounded cross-modality contrast drawn within the image slice (see §3). Yet the connection between what an LLM *says* and what the KG *contains* has not been made visually inspectable, and — more importantly — the *cause* of a grounding failure is not separable from its symptom.

This project builds an interactive visual-analytics instrument that makes grounding inspectable **and** turns grounding failure into something an analyst can manipulate and measure under controlled conditions, rather than merely observe.

## 2. Core framing and scientific position

The project's spine is a single phenomenon: **absence-induced hallucination** — fabrication that occurs when the evidence needed to ground a claim is not in front of the model. We decompose this phenomenon by the **modality of the missing evidence**:

- **Content-absence** — the entity's non-structural content is missing, even though structural triples are present. The **primary** content modality is **textual** (entity descriptions / glosses / abstracts), because text content reliably carries query-relevant facts, anchored on the **books** slice. **Images are a gated second content modality, admitted only where they demonstrably encode queryable facts**: general-purpose KG images (Wikidata P18, and book cover/identity images) are *decorative* and excluded. The image modality is realized on a **second, image-bearing slice — primarily visual artworks (paintings, Wikidata Q3305213)**, where the non-redundant signal is **relational/compositional** (e.g. *the violin is held, not played*; *a landmark is behind the figure*; spatial layout) — facts the flat `depicts` (P180) cannot express even when it lists the depicted *entities*. **Biological-taxa range-maps (P181)** — whose spatial-range non-redundancy is empirically *verified* (§10) — are retained as a **de-risked fallback** if the artwork validity gate fails. The image axis is **gated behind the books milestone (M-BOOKS) and curtailable**; the books spine stands without it.
- **Knowledge-absence** — the structural fact is not in the model's context, whether because it is absent from the KG (incompleteness) or present but not retrieved (retrieval-locality). These two share an identical model-facing symptom — the fact is simply not in context — and are therefore treated as a single failure mode rather than two axes.

The central question is whether the *modality* of the missing evidence changes the model's grounding behaviour, and whether an analyst can see, manipulate, and repair this through a visual interface. This framing makes "multimedia" load-bearing rather than decorative: the experiment manipulates each **evidence modality — structural knowledge, textual content, and image content** — and measures its effect on grounding. Because the two content modalities are richest on different slices (text on books, image on the artwork/taxa slice), the primary comparison is **within-modality, reported per slice**, anchored by **knowledge-absence (present on both slices)**; an unconfounded *cross-modality* contrast is a **second step**, drawn only within the image slice where one entity carries all three (see RQ2, §11).

**Critical design constraint — content/structure non-redundancy.** The comparison is only meaningful if the textual content carries facts the triples do *not* already encode. If descriptions merely paraphrase the triples, ablating content changes nothing the model cannot recover from structure, and the central comparison nulls out. The domain slice must therefore be chosen so that some questions depend on *content-only* facts (present in the description, absent from every triple). This is verified empirically before the design is locked (see §10).

### Relationship to prior work (what we adopt, extend, and differentiate from)

- **Claim-level attribution is adopted in spirit, then reimplemented.** KGR [1] decomposes a response into atomic claims and verifies them against KG triples, and VeGraph [6] represents claims as triplets and verifies them iteratively. None ships reusable **KG-grounded** code for our setting (GraphEval [4] is document/NLI-grounded with no public code; VeGraph is Wikipedia-derived), so we **reimplement** the KGR-style claim-attribution pipeline and **extend** it from a two-way (supported / unsupported) judgement to the three-way **retrieved / reasoned-supportable / fabricated** distinction. We also **replace KGR's LLM verification stage with a deterministic entailment gate over symbolically selected evidence**, so generation is the only stochastic stage and classifier error is calibrated separately.
- **The reasoning-vs-retrieval distinction is motivated by mechanism.** "Why LLMs Hallucinate on Structured Knowledge" [7] shows mechanistically that, when grounding evidence is absent from context, models revert to parametric memory. [7] studies the model's **internal states**; our tool does not measure that mechanism — it makes **claim supportability against the KG legible** per claim, which [7] motivates but does not amount to a measurement of the internal process.
- **Interactive KG repair exists; measuring which repairs matter does not.** CogMG [5] already performs query → detect-gap → complete-triple → update-KG → improve-answer with a human-in-the-loop interface. We differentiate by measuring **repair leverage** — how many downstream claims change status per repair — which CogMG does not.
- **Visual source attribution has been done for documents, not for graph structure.** VISA [2] draws bounding boxes on document screenshots to attribute answers to evidence. We are the **knowledge-graph analogue**: attribution to triples and subgraph paths rather than image regions.
- **The claim-extraction and retrofitting pipeline is inherited.** KGR [1] decomposes responses into atomic claims and verifies/retrofits them against KG triples. We turn its batch verification loop into an interactive, inspectable one and add the **reasoned-supportable** (reasoned-over-a-path) category it collapses.
- **Interaction and justification design follows the KG-visual-analytics roadmap.** Li et al. [3] find that practitioners want LLM outputs justified against the KG, with a two-panel design linking response text to verified subgraph structure — directly informing our interface.

## 3. Research questions

The original three questions are retained, each reframed to the honest, defensible register established during scoping.

**RQ1 — Where do LLMs hallucinate, and can it be made legible?**
Can claim-level attribution against the knowledge graph reliably separate **retrieved** (a direct triple supports the claim), **reasoned-supportable** (a multi-hop path supports it), and **fabricated** (no supporting evidence) statements, and can a visual interface make this distinction legible to a non-expert? *We make claim **supportability** legible — whether the evidence to ground the claim exists in the graph — not the model's internal reasoning process. On the image slice this becomes **multi-layer attribution** — each claim attributed to a KG triple, textual content, visual content, or none (the knowledge-graph analogue of VISA [2]). Novelty lies in the visual legibility and the three-way split, not in the attribution algorithm.*

**RQ2 — Which KG items does a query depend on?**
*(a) Observationally — support-frequency.* Across N stochastic generations of the same query, which KG items (triplets and content nodes) does grounding route through? Support-frequency — the fraction of the N runs in which an item lies on the support path of at least one grounded claim (§6) — measures **observational importance** ("how often grounding routes through this item"), explicitly **not** causal leverage.
*(b) Causally — does the absence of evidence change hallucination, and does it depend on the evidence's modality?*
*Phase A (minimal, done first).* For each evidence modality independently — **textual content** (description, books slice), **image content** (relational visual facts on the artwork slice; taxa range-maps as fallback), and **structural knowledge** (triples, both slices) — does withholding that evidence from the model's context measurably raise the fabrication rate on queries about that entity? The result is reported as the **shift in the claim-status distribution** across the conditions {full, content-withheld, knowledge-withheld}, not as a single leverage scalar (the report may state the difference of means). Effects are reported **per slice**; because content modality is confounded with domain across slices, no head-to-head text-vs-image contrast is claimed here, and **knowledge-absence — run identically on both slices — serves as a shared reference manipulation (establishing that each slice responds to absence) rather than a cross-modality comparand**. The image-content axis is conditional on its validity gate (non-redundancy + visual-probe error, §10).
*Phase B (second step, time-permitting).* On the **image slice** (artwork, or taxa fallback), where one entity carries image, description, and triples, run all three ablations on the **same entities** for an *unconfounded* cross-modality contrast — with the caveat that these descriptions are generic, so a weak text effect there reflects content-poverty, not modality. **If time is short, Phase B is curtailed first, with no loss to the Phase-A core.** *Framed as controlled manipulation, not prediction, to avoid engineering a correlation.*
*Agreement (planned analysis).* Do the two views of importance agree — are high-support-frequency items (a) also those whose withholding induces the largest distribution shift (b)? Assessed at modality level on all runs, and per-item on selected queries only (per-item withholding multiplies the run budget). A high-frequency item whose absence induces no shift (a parametrically known fact) is itself a finding, not a failure.

**RQ3 — Which repairs matter? (repair leverage)**
When an analyst restores missing evidence (adds a missing triple or restores missing content) and **re-runs**, *how many* claims flip from **fabricated to grounded** — i.e., are some gaps high-leverage (resolving a cascade of dependent claims) while others are leaves? Repair leverage is this **count of flipped claims** per repair. *Differentiates from CogMG via the leverage metric.*

## 4. Intended contributions

1. An **interactive visual-analytics instrument** that attributes each LLM claim to its supporting KG structure (and, on the image slice, to a KG / textual / visual / none **multi-layer** source) and classifies it as retrieved, reasoned-supportable, or fabricated, made legible through coordinated views.
2. A **controlled decomposition of absence-induced hallucination by evidence modality** — measuring **per slice** whether withholding textual content, image content, or structural knowledge changes grounding behaviour on curated KG slices (a text-rich books slice and a multimodal image slice — artwork-primary, taxa-fallback), reported as **shifts in the claim-status distribution** across conditions, with an **unconfounded within-image-slice cross-modality contrast as a second step** where one entity carries all three modalities.
3. A **repair-leverage measure** that quantifies which missing pieces of the KG, when restored interactively, resolve the most downstream fabrication.

## 5. Method (pointwise)

1. **KG construction.** Take a bounded domain slice of **live Wikidata** (entities + triples + textual descriptions; general-purpose P18 images excluded as decorative — a *dedicated* fact-bearing image axis is added on a second slice, below), frozen as a local graph with full ground-truth control. Live Wikidata is chosen over frozen scraped-image Freebase sets (MMKG/FB15k-IMG) because it avoids link rot, is enrichable, has reliably fact-bearing descriptions, and matches the structural lineage of KGR and CogMG. The domain slice is **books (Wikidata `Q571`)**, selected empirically via the content/structure-overlap check (§10): book descriptions frequently carry content-only facts the triples omit (genre, form, tradition/affiliation, scope, descriptive role — e.g. "key text in the Kagyu tradition of Tibetan Buddhism," "53-volume Greek anthology"), whereas mature domains such as chemical compounds showed the description merely paraphrasing the triples. Entities are sampled within a **sitelink band** (roughly 5–40 sitelinks): obscure enough that descriptions still add information, well-formed enough that the structure layer is trustworthy — avoiding both the auto-imported junk tail and the over-documented famous works whose triples subsume their descriptions. Produce a **KG-full** (ground truth) and an **ablation manifest** specifying, per experiment, which content fields (descriptions) and which triples are removed. The manifest is fixed before results are inspected. A **second slice supplies the image-content axis (§2, §7), primarily visual artworks** (paintings, `Q3305213`, with image P18 + `depicts` P180) sampled in the same sitelink band; the non-redundant signal is the **relational/compositional** facts the flat P180 cannot express. **Biological-taxa range-maps (P181), filtered to taxa whose range is not already a structured triple** — non-redundant by construction, empirically verified — are the de-risked **fallback** if the artwork validity gate fails. The image axis is gated post-M-BOOKS. The pipeline distinguishes two sets. **Evidence** is what the generator may see — triples, description, and the slice image — and is what a perturbation withholds. The **grading reference** is what the classifier always grades against: KG-full triples plus a small curated set of content-only labels (content-only textual facts for books; hand-labelled visual facts for the image slice — artwork relational facts, or taxa map facts). The image is *evidence*, not a label, so image claims are graded against the curated label rather than the raster. The live pull falls back to the independent **QLever** endpoint when WDQS is degraded.
2. **Question bank.** A fixed set of questions spanning one-hop (retrieval), multi-hop (reasoning), and questions targeting ablated entities, with difficulty tiers mirroring KGR's Simple/complex/multi-hop structure. For the content-absence axis specifically, questions target the content-only fact types that book descriptions reliably carry but triples omit — genre/form ("is this a play or a novel?"), tradition/affiliation ("what tradition is this text central to?"), scope/structure ("how many volumes?"), and descriptive role. This ensures the content-absence axis can produce signal rather than nulling out.
3. **Answer generation.** The model answers **in-context**: the (possibly ablated) evidence for the target entity — triples, description, and range-map image where in scope — is assembled into the prompt, and the LLM/VLM produces a chain-of-thought draft from that context (precomputed and stored for demo safety). A perturbation removes evidence from this **generation context** (it does not alter the grading reference); the image axis uses a VLM.
4. **Claim extraction.** KGR-style decomposition into atomic claims.
5. **Grounding & classification.** Per claim: entity-link to KG nodes; a **single piece of reference evidence that entails the claim — a direct triple *or* a content fact (description / curated image-fact label)** → *retrieved* (the modality of that evidence is recorded); else a multi-hop path (up to *k* hops) that **entails** the claim → *reasoned-supportable*; else *fabricated*. Entailment is **value-sensitive** — a claim asserting a value the evidence contradicts or omits fails the gate and is *fabricated*, so a merely-existing triple/path is **not** mislabeled — and is checked by an NLI gate against the serialized reference evidence. Image-content claims are graded the same way against their **curated label, not the raster** (the fixed-template visual probe is used to construct/verify those labels, not to grade claims). The attribution **reimplements the KGR-style pipeline** (§2) extended from two-way to this three-way split, and grades against the **grading reference**, never the ablated context.
6. **Coverage & ablation — two perturbation layers.** Per entity, record content completeness (description present; image present where applicable) and structural degree. The instrument has **two distinct layers, with distinct grading semantics**: **(a) withhold-from-context** (the RQ2 absence experiment) hides content or structural evidence from the **generation context only**; the item **stays in the grading reference**, so classification grades against the **full** reference. This is the controlled absence-induced-hallucination manipulation, and its result is the **shift in the claim-status distribution** across conditions {full, content-withheld, knowledge-withheld} — there is no leverage scalar. **(b) edit-the-KG** (gap-repair / free exploration) genuinely adds or removes a triple or node content from the KG itself, **changing the ground truth**; classification then grades against the **current (edited)** reference. **Withhold-from-context never changes the grading reference; edit-the-KG deliberately changes it** — both are graded against the current reference; the difference is whether the edit touched the reference. **Knowledge-absence is realized as withholding triples from the generation context** (covering both incompleteness and retrieval-locality, §2).
7. **Repair loop (live).** Using the edit-the-KG layer: an analyst restores missing evidence (e.g. a true claim is fabricated because the KG lacks the triple); the analyst adds the triple, **re-runs**, and the claim becomes grounded. **Repair leverage** = the **count of claims that flip from fabricated to grounded** on restore + re-run, logged per repair.
8. **Measurement-error accounting.** The grounding classifier's own error rate is quantified on a held-out hand-labelled sample, **reported per modality path — the textual NLI gate and the visual probe separately** (the visual path is expected to be noisier) — so all downstream numbers are interpretable. Because the image axis is graded against a curated hand-labelled reference rather than the KG, those map labels are **double-labelled / spot-checked** so the reference is not a single unverified judgement.

## 6. Interface (three coordinated panels)

- **Answer panel** — the LLM response with each claim colour-coded by status (retrieved / reasoned-supportable [UI label *Supportable*] / fabricated); **hue encodes status** (one fixed palette used everywhere). The analyst can **filter by the three grades** and **select several claims at once** (distinguished by outline + badge, not hue), and clicking a claim highlights its support in the graph.
- **Subgraph panel** — the local multimodal subgraph (entity images/text), with direct-support triples and multi-hop supporting paths highlighted in matching colours; claim nodes are shown with their **1st-degree neighbourhood** (under a node cap), and tapping a node zooms to it and opens an **entity-detail view showing the entity image**.
- **Analytics panel** — operating in **two modes**. In **single-run** mode (one generated answer), it shows that one run's **status percentages** with **no SE** (it is a single sample), alongside the per-claim support-path highlight and per-claim status. In **multi-run** mode (re-running the query N times, N selectable, default 20), it shows **(a)** the **status distribution as mean +/- SE** of the per-run fraction of claims that are retrieved / reasoned-supportable / fabricated (answer-level per-run fractions, then mean and SE across runs), and **(b)** a **support-frequency** read — for each KG node and each triplet, the fraction of the N runs in which it was used (lies on the support path of at least one grounded claim that run) to ground a claim — surfaced as node-size and edge-weight on the subgraph. Support-frequency is **observational importance** ("how often grounding routes through this item"), explicitly **not** causal leverage. The panel also shows **modality coverage**. Repair history carries the **repair-leverage count** (§5.7). An always-visible **per-modality classifier-error indicator** (§5.8) realises the Trust pillar.

The defining interaction is the **controlled repair loop**: spot a fabricated claim → inspect the gap → restore the missing evidence → re-run → watch the claim's status change and the analytics update. The interaction set realises an **Overview → Inspection → Repair** cycle and is designed *following the science of interaction* (Pike et al. 2009 [10]), with the low-level operations drawn from the Yi et al. 2007 categories (select, connect, filter, abstract/elaborate). This realises the sensemaking cycle (Pirolli–Card) with each repair as one iteration; in Brehmer–Munzner terms the analyst's task is *discover* (where grounding fails) and *produce* (repair), with the subgraph as the *how*. Each repair is one turn of the **knowledge-generation cycle** (Sacha et al. 2014 [11]). The interface maps onto the course's multimedia-analytics model (Worring et al. [12]): its **Outputs / Process / Knowledge / Trust** UI pillars are a cross-cutting lens over the three panels (Process = the in-answer verification trace; Trust = the error indicator), with the Wikidata KG as an Expert Module and the grounding pipeline as the Analysis agent. On the image slice the answer/subgraph panels also surface **multi-layer attribution** (KG / textual / visual / none) and show the entity image.

## 7. Extensible-by-design architecture

The minimal core instruments **one comparison** — content-absence vs. knowledge-absence — fully and rigorously. Extensibility is bought with exactly three interfaces, not a framework:

- **Perturbation-as-interface.** The pipeline consumes a KG plus a *perturbation* object. The minimal core fully instruments **text-content-absence vs. knowledge-absence** on the books slice; the same seam additionally carries an **image-content-absence** axis on the image slice (artwork-primary, taxa-fallback), **gated** — reported only if its per-domain non-redundancy check and visual-probe error accounting hold (§10). Further axes (entity-linking, conflict/staleness) are new perturbation objects added without rewriting the pipeline.
- **Uniform per-claim structured log.** Every claim is logged as (claim, linked entities, grounding path, status, active perturbation), plus the classifier's error rate. Every future axis writes into the same log, so analytics and any later factorial analysis come almost free.
- **Control-generated-from-available-perturbations UI.** The interface renders perturbation controls from the registered perturbation types, so adding an axis adds a control automatically.

**Deferred and named (not built):** the multi-axis factorial; additional failure axes (entity-linking, conflict/staleness); and — stated as an explicit scope boundary — **live diagnosis**. The tool supports *controlled* manipulation (analyst ablates a known entity and observes the effect); it does **not** claim to identify the cause of an arbitrary unseen hallucination, because content-absence and knowledge-absence are output-indistinguishable without the ground-truth of the manipulation that only exists in the controlled setting.

## 8. Evaluation design

A dual approach, per the course's evaluation lecture:

- **Analytic / simulated (primary evidence).** Quantitative results from the fixed question bank — claim-status distributions under content-ablation vs. knowledge-absence, before/after repair fabrication counts, and the repair-leverage counts per repair (defined in §5.7). The image-content-ablation axis is reported as a **gated** result — validity-conditional on its non-redundancy check and visual-probe error accounting (§5.8, §10). Runs without recruited users.
- **Qualitative (illustrative).** 2–3 end-to-end walkthrough case studies showing a fabrication caught and repaired, in the spirit of KGR's multi-hop case study; an informal think-aloud with a few peers if time permits.

A full user study is stated as future work — standard and defensible for a VIS short-paper scope. The classifier-error accounting (§5.8) is reported so the analytic results are trustworthy.

## 9. Implementation

Plotly/Dash, built on the course starter repository; code at https://github.com/erlichon/ivg-kg. Three coordinated `dcc.Graph`/custom components sharing a `dcc.Store`; a precomputed store holding answers + claims + groundings keyed by question (demo-safe, instant); NetworkX backing the subgraph and path search; one live callback for repair-regeneration (a single cheap LLM/VLM call on a small context); the image axis (gated, post-M-BOOKS) uses a local open VLM for image-grounded generation over the artwork slice (taxa fallback), and a fixed-template binary visual probe (not free-form VLM judging) to construct/verify the curated image labels that image-content claims are then graded against; the specific model and probe are in the image implementation specs (`spec/SPEC-image-artwork.md`, `spec/SPEC-image-taxa.md`), with the books core in `spec/SPEC-text.md`. The subgraph component (path highlighting + node content) is the part most likely to require going beyond off-the-shelf components, which the demo rubric rewards.

**Subgraph rendering — open sub-decision.** Evaluate Dash-native options (Dash Cytoscape, or Plotly network rendering) against a heavier JS SDK (e.g. yFiles, which is purpose-built for interactive KG visualisation but is commercial and non-trivial to embed in Dash). Default to the Dash-native path unless its path-highlighting / dynamic-layout limits prove blocking; the JS SDK is a fallback, not a starting point, given the Plotly/Dash course requirement.

## 10. Scope, risks, and honest boundaries

- **Data dependency (resolved).** The substrate is **live Wikidata**: a **books (`Q571`)** slice for text-content vs. knowledge, and a gated **image slice — primarily visual artworks (paintings)**, with **taxa range-maps (`P181`)** as a verified fallback — for the image-content axis. General-purpose P18 images are decorative and excluded, but artwork *relational* facts (and taxa range maps) are fact-bearing image content (see the non-redundancy discussion below); no off-the-shelf MMKG suits QA, as the existing ones target entity alignment / link prediction. The content/structure-overlap check confirmed books carry content-only facts the triples omit, where mature domains (e.g. chemical compounds) did not. The live pull falls back to the independent **QLever** endpoint when WDQS is degraded. If a slice proves insufficient at scale, the fallback is another sparsely-structured Wikidata domain or knowledge-absence alone.
- **Image-axis cost and validity (gated).** Genuine image non-redundancy means the fact is *not* in the triples, so image claims are graded against a **small hand-labelled set**, not the KG; VLMs hallucinate (mitigated by the fixed-template visual probe, not free-form judging); range maps are often SVG (rasterised for the VLM). The non-redundancy gate is *verified* for taxa and **must be re-checked for artworks (the relational layer) before locking**. **Fallback chain (post-M-BOOKS, curtailable):** artwork first → if its validity gate fails and time remains, the verified **taxa range-map** fallback → else the image axis is dropped, leaving the fully-measured **text-content vs. knowledge-absence** books spine with no core loss.
- **Obscurity-vs-reliability tension (new, manage via sitelink band).** The content/structure gap is driven by entity obscurity: the entities where content-absence shows an effect (sparse triples, rich descriptions) are also where the KG is least reliable and the LLM has least parametric knowledge. Mitigated by sampling within a sitelink band (§5.1) — obscure enough for descriptive signal, well-formed enough for trustworthy structure — but it remains a balance to monitor during entity selection.
- **The "reasoned-supportable" category is the thorniest measurement.** Retrieved and fabricated are clean; "reasoned-supportable" (a valid multi-hop path that entails the claim) is hard to distinguish from plausibly fabricated. The entailment gate (§5.5) mitigates this — a path that does not entail the claim is *not* labelled reasoned-supportable — and §5.8 reports this category's error rate explicitly. The three-way split — our differentiator from a two-way supported/unsupported judgement — rests on the hardest-to-measure category. Budget accordingly.
- **Curated-KG validity.** With a curated KG and designed questions, results are findings on a controlled testbed, not universal laws. RQ2 is framed as controlled manipulation, not prediction, and the ablation manifest is fixed before inspection, to avoid engineering the observed effect.
- **Thin-but-extensible is still thin.** The minimal core must be fully measured on its one comparison; extensibility buys future axes, not permission to be preliminary on the first.
