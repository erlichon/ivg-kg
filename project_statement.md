## 1. Problem

Large language models produce fluent, confident answers, but a reader cannot tell *how* any given statement was arrived at. Three failure-relevant cases are conflated in the output: facts the model **retrieved** from reliable structured knowledge, facts it obtained by **reasoning** over several pieces of structured knowledge, and facts it **fabricated**. (What a tool can measure post-hoc is *supportability* — whether the evidence needed to ground a claim exists in the graph — so from §3 onward the middle category is named **reasoned-supportable**. The three statuses are **evidential, not process claims**: "retrieved" is shorthand for *directly supported by a single triple/content fact*, not an assertion about what the model actually did — the tool measures **supportability**, not the model's internal process.) Knowledge graphs (KGs) store high-quality factual triples about entities; *multimodal* KGs additionally attach images and textual descriptions to each entity, making them a rich substrate for multimedia analytics. A text-rich **books** slice anchors the textual-content modality; an **image slice** — primarily **visual artworks** (paintings), with biological-taxa range-maps as a verified fallback — instantiates the image modality, where the image carries facts the triples do not. The study examines each evidence modality **within its slice** (the promised, within-modality claim); an unconfounded cross-modality contrast within the image slice is **future upside, not promised** (see §3). Yet the connection between what an LLM *says* and what the KG *contains* has not been made visually inspectable, and — more importantly — the *cause* of a grounding failure is not separable from its symptom.

This project builds an **MMA4AI instrument** (in the course's lecture-6 sense): an interactive visual-analytics tool whose *one main action is analyze* — it **probes**, rather than prompts, an LLM's knowledge-graph grounding so an analyst can **understand and calibrate trust** in the model before acting. (Whether the instrument actually lets an analyst *calibrate trust* and reads as *legible to a non-expert* is the **design intent** of the instrument, pending the future user study — not an established result of this work.) It makes grounding inspectable **and** turns grounding failure into something the analyst can manipulate, measure, and repair under controlled conditions, rather than merely observe. The contribution is the *instrument*, not an ML result: ivg-kg grounds each generated claim against a frozen Wikidata slice, classifies it *retrieved / Supportable / fabricated*, pairs every verdict with a faithful support-path attribution, and lets the analyst repair the knowledge gaps that cause fabrication.

## 2. Core framing and scientific position

Framed as an MMA4AI instrument (above), the project's *scientific* spine is a single phenomenon the instrument is built to probe: **absence-induced hallucination** — fabrication that occurs when the evidence needed to ground a claim is not in front of the model. We decompose this phenomenon by the **modality of the missing evidence** — the manipulated variable of the study, which is what makes the multimodal axis **instrumented and analyzed** rather than decorative. The robust, promised claim is **within-modality**: each modality's absence induces fabrication within its own slice. The **cross-modality contrast** ("modality M matters more than M'") is Phase-B / future upside (§23, §53-54) and is **explicitly not promised**; what is committed is the image axis itself, not the contrast across modalities.

- **Content-absence** — the entity's non-structural content is missing, even though structural triples are present. The **primary** content modality is **textual** (entity descriptions / glosses / abstracts), because text content reliably carries query-relevant facts, anchored on the **books** slice. **Images are a gated second content modality, admitted only where they demonstrably encode queryable facts**: general-purpose KG images (Wikidata P18, and book cover/identity images) are *decorative* and excluded. The image modality is realized on a **second, image-bearing slice — primarily visual artworks (paintings, Wikidata Q3305213)**, where the non-redundant signal is **relational/compositional** (e.g. *the violin is held, not played*; *a landmark is behind the figure*; spatial layout) — facts the flat `depicts` (P180) cannot express even when it lists the depicted *entities*. **Biological-taxa range-maps (P181)** — whose spatial-range non-redundancy is empirically *verified* (§10) — are the **de-risked fallback that guarantees an image axis** if the artwork non-redundancy gate fails. The image axis is **committed, not optional**: it is *sequenced* after the books milestone (M-BOOKS), not contingent on it. The validity gate chooses *which* image domain (artwork or taxa), never *whether* to do image; multimodality is a commitment, sequenced after text.
- **Knowledge-absence** — the structural fact is not in the model's context, whether because it is absent from the KG (incompleteness) or present but not retrieved (retrieval-locality). These two share an identical model-facing symptom — the fact is simply not in context — and are therefore treated as a single failure mode rather than two axes.

The central question is whether the *modality* of the missing evidence changes the model's grounding behaviour, and whether an analyst can see, manipulate, and repair this through a visual interface. This framing makes "multimedia" **instrumented and analyzed** rather than decorative: the experiment manipulates each **evidence modality — structural knowledge, textual content, and image content** — and measures its effect on grounding. Because the two content modalities are richest on different slices (text on books, image on the artwork/taxa slice), the **promised, within-modality** comparison is **reported per slice** (each modality's absence induces fabrication within its slice), anchored by **knowledge-absence (present on both slices)**; an unconfounded *cross-modality* contrast is a **second step / future upside, not promised**, drawn only within the image slice where one entity carries all three (see RQ2, §11).

**Critical design constraint — content/structure non-redundancy.** The comparison is only meaningful if the textual content carries facts the triples do *not* already encode. If descriptions merely paraphrase the triples, ablating content changes nothing the model cannot recover from structure, and the central comparison nulls out. The domain slice must therefore be chosen so that some questions depend on *content-only* facts (present in the description, absent from every triple). This is verified empirically before the design is locked (see §10).

### Relationship to prior work (what we adopt, extend, and differentiate from)

- **Novelty honesty — we do not invent causal evidence attribution.** **ContextCite** (Cohen-Wang, Shah, Georgiev, Madry [13]) already operationalises the *causal* importance of context via ablation — a sparse linear surrogate over random context-ablations yielding a per-source causal effect — so the causal-importance half of our analysis is **not novel as a primitive**. ContextCite differs by working at **text-span**, not **KG-triple**, granularity; reports a single attribution score, not an **observational-vs-causal agreement**; ships no deterministic KG verifier, no visual-analytics interface, and no repair loop. Likewise, the **cited-but-not-relied-upon** phenomenon is old: the lineage runs from **Jain & Wallace, "Attention is not Explanation"** [14] (attention weights are not faithful explanations of what a model relied on) through **Wallat et al.** [15] (up to 57% of RAG citations are post-hoc rationalisations). We therefore claim novelty **only** in the **composition at KG-triple granularity**: an observational (support-frequency) -vs- causal (absence-shift) **agreement taxonomy over individual KG facts** — load-bearing / redundant-scaffold / hidden-dependency / inert (§3, RQ2) — produced by a **deterministic** verifier, explored **interactively**, and closed by a **repair loop**. The delivered novelty is the **instrument and the taxonomy-as-a-lens** (present in the UI by construction), not the attribution primitive, the phenomenon, or the *populated* quadrant (which is the cuttable finding, §4-C3).
- **Claim-level attribution is adopted in spirit, then reimplemented.** KGR [1] decomposes a response into atomic claims and verifies them against KG triples, and VeGraph [6] represents claims as triplets and verifies them iteratively. None ships reusable **KG-grounded** code for our setting (GraphEval [4] is document/NLI-grounded with no public code; VeGraph is Wikipedia-derived), so we **reimplement** the KGR-style claim-attribution pipeline and **extend** it from a two-way (supported / unsupported) judgement to the three-way **retrieved / reasoned-supportable / fabricated** distinction. We also **replace KGR's LLM verification stage with a deterministic entailment gate over symbolically selected evidence**, so generation is the only stochastic stage and classifier error is calibrated separately.
- **The reasoning-vs-retrieval distinction is motivated by mechanism.** "Why LLMs Hallucinate on Structured Knowledge" [7] shows mechanistically that, when grounding evidence is absent from context, models revert to parametric memory. [7] studies the model's **internal states**; our tool does not measure that mechanism — it makes **claim supportability against the KG legible** per claim, which [7] motivates but does not amount to a measurement of the internal process.
- **Interactive KG repair exists; measuring which repairs matter does not.** CogMG [5] already performs query → detect-gap → complete-triple → update-KG → improve-answer with a human-in-the-loop interface. We differentiate by measuring **repair leverage** — how many downstream claims change status per repair — which CogMG does not. **repair_leverage is a standalone delivered novelty**: it ships with the instrument (C1/RQ3) and survives even if the C3 agreement sweep is only a pilot.
- **Visual source attribution has been done for documents, not for graph structure.** VISA [2] draws bounding boxes on document screenshots to attribute answers to evidence. We are the **knowledge-graph analogue**: attribution to triples and subgraph paths rather than image regions.
- **Multimedia novelty honesty — we claim the composition, not the primitives.** As on the text side, the multimedia contribution's novelty is the **composition**: an interactive VA instrument that does **per-claim KG grounding + multi-layer attribution (KG-triple / text / image / none) + image-content-absence ablation + KG-repair** as one coupled tool — not any single primitive. We position against the closest prior art. *Graphing the Truth* [9] is the nearest visual-analytics competitor but is **text-only** (it lacks the entire image axis). VISA [2] is visual source attribution for documents. The **image-withholding ablation *mechanic* is prior art** (VDGD [22]; M3ID) — we do **not** claim it. Multimodal citation/attribution also exists (MCiteBench [23]). State plainly: **the image-ablation mechanic is not novel; the integration is.** The **guaranteed floor** is taxa range-maps (verified ~62% non-redundant, §10 — one fact type) plus multi-layer attribution; **artwork relational facts are securable upside**, gated on a **pre-registered non-redundancy check that has not yet been run** (§10).
- **The claim-extraction and retrofitting pipeline is inherited.** KGR [1] decomposes responses into atomic claims and verifies/retrofits them against KG triples. We turn its batch verification loop into an interactive, inspectable one and add the **reasoned-supportable** (reasoned-over-a-path) category it collapses.
- **Interaction and justification design follows the KG-visual-analytics roadmap.** Li et al. [3] find that practitioners want LLM outputs justified against the KG, with a two-panel design linking response text to verified subgraph structure — directly informing our interface.

## 3. Requirements and research questions

**Target audience:** *an analyst calibrating trust in an LLM's knowledge-graph grounding* — not an ML researcher inspecting weights, but a decision-maker who must judge whether a generated answer can be relied upon. Following the lecture-6 pattern that every MMA4AI system is requirements-driven (GNNLens R1->R2->R3), the instrument's design requirements map onto the **Overview -> Inspection -> Repair** spine:

- **R1 (Overview).** Give the analyst an at-a-glance read of *where* grounding fails across an answer (and across N runs): per-claim status, the support-frequency map of which KG items grounding routes through, and the instrument's own error first (the Trust strip).
- **R2 (Inspection).** Let the analyst drill from a flagged claim to *why* the verdict was reached — the faithful support-path attribution (Provenance Card), the why-fabricated slot-diff (value-mismatch vs missing-fact), and the epistemic level of the evidence (observed / intervened / n=1).
- **R3 (Repair).** Let the analyst *act*: restore missing evidence, re-run, and watch status change — measuring which repairs resolve the most downstream fabrication (repair_leverage).

The three research questions below are the *scientific* probes the instrument enables; each is reframed to the honest, defensible register established during scoping.

**RQ1 — Where do LLMs hallucinate, and can it be made legible?**
Can claim-level attribution against the knowledge graph reliably separate **retrieved** (a direct triple supports the claim), **reasoned-supportable** (a multi-hop path supports it), and **fabricated** (no supporting evidence) statements, and can a visual interface make this distinction legible to a non-expert? *(Making the distinction **legible to a non-expert** is the interface's **design intent**, to be assessed by the future user study, not an established result.)* *We make claim **supportability** legible — whether the evidence to ground the claim exists in the graph — not the model's internal reasoning process. On the image slice this becomes **multi-layer attribution** — each claim attributed to a KG triple, textual content, visual content, or none (the knowledge-graph analogue of VISA [2]). Novelty lies in the visual legibility and the three-way split, not in the attribution algorithm.*

**RQ2 — Which KG items does a query depend on?**
*(a) Observationally — support-frequency.* Across N stochastic generations of the same query, which KG items (triplets and content nodes) does grounding route through? Support-frequency — the fraction of the N runs in which an item lies on the support path of at least one grounded claim (§6) — measures **observational importance** ("how often grounding routes through this item"), explicitly **not** causal leverage.
*(b) Causally — does the absence of evidence change hallucination, and does it depend on the evidence's modality?*
*Phase A (minimal, done first).* For each evidence modality independently — **textual content** (description, books slice), **image content** (relational visual facts on the artwork slice; taxa range-maps as fallback), and **structural knowledge** (triples, both slices) — does withholding that evidence from the model's context measurably raise the fabrication rate on queries about that entity? The result is reported as the **shift in the claim-status distribution** across the conditions {full, content-withheld, knowledge-withheld}, not as a single leverage scalar (the report may state the difference of means). Effects are reported **per slice**; because content modality is confounded with domain across slices, no head-to-head text-vs-image contrast is claimed here, and **knowledge-absence — run identically on both slices — serves as a shared reference manipulation (establishing that each slice responds to absence) rather than a cross-modality comparand**. The image-content axis is conditional on its validity gate (non-redundancy + visual-probe error, §10).
*Phase B (second step, time-permitting).* On the **image slice** (artwork, or taxa fallback), where one entity carries image, description, and triples, run all three ablations on the **same entities** for an *unconfounded* cross-modality contrast — with the caveat that these descriptions are generic, so a weak text effect there reflects content-poverty, not modality. **If time is short, the *cross-modality contrast* (Phase B) is curtailed first, with no loss to the Phase-A core — which already includes the committed image-content axis;** Phase B trims the unconfounded comparison, not the image modality. *Framed as controlled manipulation, not prediction, to avoid engineering a correlation.*
*Agreement (planned analysis — the C3 quadrant).* Do the two views of importance agree — are high-support-frequency items (a) also those whose withholding induces the largest distribution shift (b)? Crossing the two axes at **KG-triple grain** yields a four-cell **agreement quadrant**: **load-bearing** (high frequency, high shift), **redundant-scaffold** (high frequency, low shift — cited but not relied upon), **hidden-dependency** (low frequency, high shift), and **inert** (low frequency, low shift). The **novelty here is the instrument plus the taxonomy-as-a-lens** — present in C1's UI by construction — **not** the *populated* quadrant, which is the cuttable empirical finding (§2, §4-C3). Assessed at modality level on all runs, and per-item on selected queries only (per-item withholding multiplies the run budget). A high-frequency item whose absence induces no shift (a parametrically known fact, i.e. a redundant-scaffold cell) is itself a finding, not a failure.

**RQ3 — Which repairs matter? (repair leverage)**
When an analyst restores missing evidence (adds a missing triple or restores missing content) and **re-runs**, *how many* claims flip from **fabricated to grounded** — i.e., are some gaps high-leverage (resolving a cascade of dependent claims) while others are leaves? Repair leverage is this **count of flipped claims** per repair. *Differentiates from CogMG via the leverage metric.*

## 4. Intended contributions

The contributions are ordered by decreasing robustness and built in this order; **C1 earns the grade even if the sweep finds nothing.**

1. **C1 — the instrument.** An **interactive MMA4AI visual-analytics instrument** that grounds each LLM claim against the KG via a **deterministic, calibrated verifier**, attributes it to its supporting KG structure (and, on the image slice, to a KG / textual / visual / none **multi-layer** source), classifies it as retrieved / reasoned-supportable (UI: *Supportable*) / fabricated, and drives the **Overview -> Inspection -> Repair** loop — including the repair loop and repair_leverage — all wrapped in the **honesty layer** (§5-6: two-tier data-agnostic trust, the "fabricated != false" overlay, the Provenance Card, and the epistemic glyph grammar). This is the contribution, and it stands independent of any empirical finding.
2. **C2 — the absence finding.** A **controlled decomposition of absence-induced hallucination by evidence modality** — the **modality of the withheld evidence (textual content / structural knowledge / image content) is the manipulated variable**, which is why multimodality is **instrumented and analyzed** here — measuring **per slice** whether withholding each modality changes grounding behaviour on curated KG slices (a text-rich books slice and a multimodal image slice — artwork-primary, taxa-fallback), reported as **shifts in the claim-status distribution** across conditions. The **promised claim is within-modality**; an **unconfounded within-image-slice cross-modality contrast** (where one entity carries all three modalities) is a **second-step / future upside, not promised** (Phase B, §3/§11).
3. **C3 — the agreement quadrant.** An **observational(support-frequency)-vs-causal(absence-shift) agreement taxonomy at KG-triple grain** — load-bearing / redundant-scaffold / hidden-dependency / inert (§3, RQ2). The **delivered novelty is the instrument plus the taxonomy-as-a-lens** (present in C1's UI by construction; the composition, not the attribution primitive, §2); the **populated quadrant is the cuttable empirical finding** — treated as upside, it must not drive the schedule and is the first to be cut to a pilot. A **delivered novelty survives even if C3 is only a pilot**: within C1/RQ3, **repair_leverage** is promoted to a **standalone delivered novelty** — it quantifies which missing pieces of the KG, when restored interactively, resolve the most downstream fabrication, differentiating from CogMG via the leverage measure (§2), and ships with the instrument independent of the C3 sweep.

## 5. Method (pointwise)

1. **KG construction.** Take a bounded domain slice of **live Wikidata** (entities + triples + textual descriptions; general-purpose P18 images excluded as decorative — a *dedicated* fact-bearing image axis is added on a second slice, below), frozen as a local graph with full ground-truth control. Live Wikidata is chosen over frozen scraped-image Freebase sets (MMKG/FB15k-IMG) because it avoids link rot, is enrichable, has reliably fact-bearing descriptions, and matches the structural lineage of KGR and CogMG. The domain slice is **books (Wikidata `Q571`)**, selected empirically via the content/structure-overlap check (§10): book descriptions frequently carry content-only facts the triples omit (genre, form, tradition/affiliation, scope, descriptive role — e.g. "key text in the Kagyu tradition of Tibetan Buddhism," "53-volume Greek anthology"), whereas mature domains such as chemical compounds showed the description merely paraphrasing the triples. Entities are sampled within a **sitelink band** (roughly 5–40 sitelinks): obscure enough that descriptions still add information, well-formed enough that the structure layer is trustworthy — avoiding both the auto-imported junk tail and the over-documented famous works whose triples subsume their descriptions. Produce a **KG-full** (ground truth) and an **ablation manifest** specifying, per experiment, which content fields (descriptions) and which triples are removed. The manifest is fixed before results are inspected. A **second slice supplies the image-content axis (§2, §7), primarily visual artworks** (paintings, `Q3305213`, with image P18 + `depicts` P180) sampled in the same sitelink band; the non-redundant signal is the **relational/compositional** facts the flat P180 cannot express. **Biological-taxa range-maps (P181), filtered to taxa whose range is not already a structured triple** — non-redundant by construction, empirically verified — are the de-risked **fallback that guarantees the image axis** if the artwork validity gate fails. The image axis is **sequenced after M-BOOKS (committed, not contingent)**; the validity gate routes artwork->taxa, it never abandons the modality. The pipeline distinguishes two sets. **Evidence** is what the generator may see — triples, description, and the slice image — and is what a perturbation withholds. The **grading reference** is what the classifier always grades against: KG-full triples plus a small curated set of content-only labels (content-only textual facts for books; hand-labelled visual facts for the image slice — artwork relational facts, or taxa map facts). The image is *evidence*, not a label, so image claims are graded against the curated label rather than the raster. The live pull falls back to the independent **QLever** endpoint when WDQS is degraded.
2. **Question bank.** A fixed set of questions spanning one-hop (retrieval), multi-hop (reasoning), and questions targeting ablated entities, with difficulty tiers mirroring KGR's Simple/complex/multi-hop structure. For the content-absence axis specifically, questions target the content-only fact types that book descriptions reliably carry but triples omit — genre/form ("is this a play or a novel?"), tradition/affiliation ("what tradition is this text central to?"), scope/structure ("how many volumes?"), and descriptive role. This ensures the content-absence axis can produce signal rather than nulling out.
3. **Answer generation.** The model answers **in-context**: the (possibly ablated) evidence for the target entity — triples, description, and range-map image where in scope — is assembled into the prompt, and the LLM/VLM produces a chain-of-thought draft from that context (precomputed and stored for demo safety). A perturbation removes evidence from this **generation context** (it does not alter the grading reference); the image axis uses a VLM.
4. **Claim extraction.** KGR-style decomposition into atomic claims.
5. **Grounding & classification.** Per claim: entity-link to KG nodes; a **single piece of reference evidence that entails the claim — a direct triple *or* a content fact (description / curated image-fact label)** → *retrieved* (the modality of that evidence is recorded); else a multi-hop path (up to *k* hops) that **entails** the claim → *reasoned-supportable*; else *fabricated*. Entailment is **value-sensitive** — a claim asserting a value the evidence contradicts or omits fails the gate and is *fabricated*, so a merely-existing triple/path is **not** mislabeled — and is checked by an NLI gate against the serialized reference evidence. Image-content claims are graded the same way against their **curated label, not the raster** (the fixed-template visual probe is used to construct/verify those labels, not to grade claims). The attribution **reimplements the KGR-style pipeline** (§2) extended from two-way to this three-way split, and grades against the **grading reference**, never the ablated context.
6. **Coverage & ablation — two perturbation layers.** Per entity, record content completeness (description present; image present where applicable) and structural degree. The instrument has **two distinct layers, with distinct grading semantics**: **(a) withhold-from-context** (the RQ2 absence experiment) hides content or structural evidence from the **generation context only**; the item **stays in the grading reference**, so classification grades against the **full** reference. This is the controlled absence-induced-hallucination manipulation, and its result is the **shift in the claim-status distribution** across conditions {full, content-withheld, knowledge-withheld} — there is no leverage scalar. **(b) edit-the-KG** (gap-repair / free exploration) genuinely adds or removes a triple or node content from the KG itself, **changing the ground truth**; classification then grades against the **current (edited)** reference. **Withhold-from-context never changes the grading reference; edit-the-KG deliberately changes it** — both are graded against the current reference; the difference is whether the edit touched the reference. **Knowledge-absence is realized as withholding triples from the generation context** (covering both incompleteness and retrieval-locality, §2).
7. **Repair loop (live).** Using the edit-the-KG layer: an analyst restores missing evidence (e.g. a true claim is fabricated because the KG lacks the triple); the analyst adds the triple, **re-runs**, and the claim becomes grounded. **Repair leverage** = the **count of claims that flip from fabricated to grounded** on restore + re-run, logged per repair.
8. **Measurement-error accounting.** The grounding classifier's own error rate is quantified on a held-out hand-labelled sample, **reported per modality path — the textual NLI gate and the visual probe separately** (the visual path is expected to be noisier) — so all downstream numbers are interpretable. Because the image axis is graded against a curated hand-labelled reference rather than the KG, those map labels are **double-labelled / spot-checked** so the reference is not a single unverified judgement.
9. **The honesty layer (the instrument's trust + interpretability + causality spine).** C1's defining property is that the instrument **shows its own error first** and never lets correlation read as causation. It has five parts:
   - **(a) Two-tier, data-agnostic trust.** *Instrument-level* (shown for **any** KG, always): an **uncalibrated reliability prior** — the NLI gate's **published benchmark accuracy** plus the **per-claim entailment margin to tau**, which is a **confidence proxy, not calibration** — no labels required. *Deployment-level* (shown when labels exist): error **calibrated on a curated gold QA set for the loaded slice** (computed and shown for the books demo) — this is the only tier for which "calibrated" is used in its strict sense (the gold-QA-set numbers). For a user's **BYO-KG**, the deployment tier **degrades gracefully** to the instrument-level uncalibrated prior plus an explicit "not calibrated to your KG" caveat and a "label N claims to calibrate" affordance.
   - **(b) The curated gold QA set does double duty:** it **calibrates the verifier** (this trust tier) **and anchors the RQ2 sweep** (C2) — "how big a sweep?" is answered by a good curated QA set, not "the whole dataset".
   - **(c) The "fabricated != false" overlay:** out-of-slice claims are hatched — *unsupported here is not the same as untrue* — so the analyst does not read absence-of-support as falsehood.
   - **(d) The Provenance Card:** the verifier's "reasoning" is a **faithful deterministic proof chain**, not a narration. It is shown **beside** the generator's chain-of-thought, which is labelled "stated, not necessarily faithful" — the fallible-vs-faithful contrast is itself a lesson and visualises system-under-test vs instrument.
   - **(e) The epistemic glyph grammar:** three glyphs that never get confused — open circle = **observational** (support-frequency), filled triangle+interval = **interventional aggregate** (the sweep), outlined triangle "n=1" = **single-sample demo** — keeping the three rigor levels of causal evidence distinct. This disciplined encoding (not a new causal method) is the project's causality contribution; it prevents the tool's worst failure mode, reading correlation as causation.

## 6. Interface (three coordinated panels)

Built by Worring's recipe (purpose+audience -> architecture -> **define scores** -> visualize -> coordinate views): the instrument's **scores/metrics** are **STATUS** (retrieved/Supportable/fabricated), **support-frequency**, **repair_leverage**, and **verifier-error**, and the panels below render them. The deterministic verifier is an **Analysis VA-Agent** whose contract is a trustworthy rationale — the **Provenance Card** (its faithful deterministic proof chain), shown beside the generator's chain-of-thought; the sampled LLM is the **Generate/Analyze agent**. The guidance posture is deliberately **Orienting + Directing, not Prescriptive** (status hues + Trust strip orient; the why-fabricated slot-diff + repair_leverage direct) — licensed by lecture 6's caution that "guidance can easily lead to bias."

- **Answer panel** — the LLM response with each claim colour-coded by status (retrieved / reasoned-supportable [UI label *Supportable*] / fabricated); **hue encodes status** (one fixed palette used everywhere). The analyst can **filter by the three grades** and **select several claims at once** (distinguished by outline + badge, not hue), and clicking a claim highlights its support in the graph.
- **Subgraph panel** — the local multimodal subgraph (entity images/text), with direct-support triples and multi-hop supporting paths highlighted in matching colours; claim nodes are shown with their **1st-degree neighbourhood** (under a node cap), and tapping a node zooms to it and opens an **entity-detail view showing the entity image**.
- **Analytics panel** — operating in **two modes**. In **single-run** mode (one generated answer), it shows that one run's **status percentages** with **no SE** (it is a single sample), alongside the per-claim support-path highlight and per-claim status. In **multi-run** mode (re-running the query N times, N selectable, default 20), it shows **(a)** the **status distribution as mean +/- SE** of the per-run fraction of claims that are retrieved / reasoned-supportable / fabricated (answer-level per-run fractions, then mean and SE across runs), and **(b)** a **support-frequency** read — for each KG node and each triplet, the fraction of the N runs in which it was used (lies on the support path of at least one grounded claim that run) to ground a claim — surfaced as node-size and edge-weight on the subgraph. Support-frequency is **observational importance** ("how often grounding routes through this item"), explicitly **not** causal leverage — and is encoded with the **open-circle epistemic glyph** to keep it distinct from interventional (sweep) and n=1 (single-run) evidence (§5.9e). The panel also shows **modality coverage**. Repair history carries the **repair_leverage count** (§5.7), bound to a size/glyph channel rather than prose. The **highest-leverage UI point is the Trust strip**: the two-tier verifier-error read (§5.9a) plus the "fabricated != false" overlay (§5.9c) must be a **visible, always-on element** on stage — the honesty layer is the headline trust contribution and must never be invisible in a screenshot.

The defining interaction is the **controlled repair loop**: spot a fabricated claim → inspect the gap → restore the missing evidence → re-run → watch the claim's status change and the analytics update. The interaction set realises an **Overview → Inspection → Repair** cycle and is designed *following the science of interaction* (Pike et al. 2009 [10]), with the low-level operations drawn from the Yi et al. 2007 categories (select, connect, filter, abstract/elaborate). This realises the sensemaking cycle (Pirolli–Card) with each repair as one iteration; in Brehmer–Munzner terms the analyst's task is *discover* (where grounding fails) and *produce* (repair), with the subgraph as the *how*. Each repair is one turn of the **knowledge-generation cycle** (Sacha et al. 2014 [11]). The interface maps onto the course's multimedia-analytics model (Worring et al. [12]): its **Outputs / Process / Knowledge / Trust** UI pillars are a cross-cutting lens over the three panels (Process = the in-answer verification trace; Trust = the always-on error strip), with the sampled LLM as the **Generate/Analyze agent** and the deterministic grounding pipeline as the **Analysis VA-Agent** whose rationale is the Provenance Card. On the image slice the answer/subgraph panels also surface **multi-layer attribution** (KG / textual / visual / none) and show the entity image. **Scope bound:** the repair loop is **human-AI teaming without RL strategy optimization** — it gathers human repairs but deliberately does not close a reinforcement-learning loop, pre-empting the "why no learning loop" question.

## 7. Extensible-by-design architecture

The minimal core instruments **one comparison** — content-absence vs. knowledge-absence — fully and rigorously. Extensibility is bought with exactly three interfaces, not a framework:

- **Perturbation-as-interface.** The pipeline consumes a KG plus a *perturbation* object. The text core fully instruments **text-content-absence vs. knowledge-absence** on the books slice (built first); the same seam carries the **committed image-content-absence** axis on the image slice (artwork-primary, taxa-fallback), sequenced as the second phase. The per-domain non-redundancy check + visual-probe error accounting (§10) is a technical checkpoint that **routes artwork->taxa**, not a switch that drops the modality. Further axes (entity-linking, conflict/staleness) are new perturbation objects added without rewriting the pipeline.
- **Uniform per-claim structured log.** Every claim is logged as (claim, linked entities, grounding path, status, active perturbation), plus the classifier's error rate. Every future axis writes into the same log, so analytics and any later factorial analysis come almost free.
- **Control-generated-from-available-perturbations UI.** The interface renders perturbation controls from the registered perturbation types, so adding an axis adds a control automatically.

**Deferred and named (not built):** the multi-axis factorial; additional failure axes (entity-linking, conflict/staleness); and — stated as an explicit scope boundary — **live diagnosis**. The tool supports *controlled* manipulation (analyst ablates a known entity and observes the effect); it does **not** claim to identify the cause of an arbitrary unseen hallucination, because content-absence and knowledge-absence are output-indistinguishable without the ground-truth of the manipulation that only exists in the controlled setting.

## 8. Evaluation design

A multi-pronged approach, per the course's evaluation lecture:

- **Analytic / simulated (primary evidence).** Quantitative results from the fixed question bank — claim-status distributions under content-ablation vs. knowledge-absence, before/after repair fabrication counts, and the repair_leverage counts per repair (defined in §5.7). The image-content-ablation axis is reported **validity-conditional** on its non-redundancy check and visual-probe error accounting (§5.8, §10) — the gate routes which image domain, not whether the axis ships. Runs without recruited users.
- **Cost-benefit / van Wijk (analytic quality).** The instrument is assessed against van Wijk's value model — the analytic **K** (knowledge gained: fabrications surfaced, dependencies revealed, repairs leveraged) against the **C** (the cost of the offline sweep + the analyst's inspection effort) — argued via the simulation evidence above, justifying the coordinated-views design over a single complex view.
- **Insight-based / North.** Insight-based reporting (North) of what an analyst learns from a walkthrough — the discoveries (a fabrication caught, a hidden-dependency triple, a high-leverage repair) treated as the unit of evaluation.
- **Instrument self-measurement.** The verifier-calibration numbers (§5.8-5.9a) are framed as the instrument **measuring its own error** — the Trust tier is not a separate evaluation but the instrument reporting its own reliability first.
- **Qualitative (illustrative).** 2-3 end-to-end walkthrough case studies showing a fabrication caught and repaired, in the spirit of KGR's multi-hop case study; an informal think-aloud with a few peers if time permits.

A full user study is stated as future work — standard and defensible for a VIS short-paper scope. The classifier-error accounting (§5.8) is reported so the analytic results are trustworthy.

## 9. Implementation

Plotly/Dash, built on the course starter repository; code at https://github.com/erlichon/ivg-kg. Three coordinated `dcc.Graph`/custom components sharing a `dcc.Store`; a precomputed store holding answers + claims + groundings keyed by question (demo-safe, instant); NetworkX backing the subgraph and path search; one live callback for repair-regeneration (a single cheap LLM/VLM call on a small context); the committed image axis (second phase, sequenced post-M-BOOKS) uses a local open VLM for image-grounded generation over the artwork slice (taxa fallback), and a fixed-template binary visual probe (not free-form VLM judging) to construct/verify the curated image labels that image-content claims are then graded against; the specific model and probe are in the image implementation specs (`spec/SPEC-image-artwork.md`, `spec/SPEC-image-taxa.md`), with the books core in `spec/SPEC-text.md`. The subgraph component (path highlighting + node content) is the part most likely to require going beyond off-the-shelf components, which the demo rubric rewards.

**Subgraph rendering — open sub-decision.** Evaluate Dash-native options (Dash Cytoscape, or Plotly network rendering) against a heavier JS SDK (e.g. yFiles, which is purpose-built for interactive KG visualisation but is commercial and non-trivial to embed in Dash). Default to the Dash-native path unless its path-highlighting / dynamic-layout limits prove blocking; the JS SDK is a fallback, not a starting point, given the Plotly/Dash course requirement.

## 10. Scope, risks, and honest boundaries

- **Data dependency (resolved).** The substrate is **live Wikidata**: a **books (`Q571`)** slice for text-content vs. knowledge, and a **committed second-phase image slice — primarily visual artworks (paintings)**, with **taxa range-maps (`P181`)** as a verified fallback — for the image-content axis. General-purpose P18 images are decorative and excluded, but artwork *relational* facts (and taxa range maps) are fact-bearing image content (see the non-redundancy discussion below); no off-the-shelf MMKG suits QA, as the existing ones target entity alignment / link prediction. The content/structure-overlap check confirmed books carry content-only facts the triples omit, where mature domains (e.g. chemical compounds) did not. The live pull falls back to the independent **QLever** endpoint when WDQS is degraded. If a slice proves insufficient at scale, the fallback is another sparsely-structured Wikidata domain or knowledge-absence alone.
- **Image-axis cost and validity (committed; gate routes the domain).** Genuine image non-redundancy means the fact is *not* in the triples, so image claims are graded against a **small hand-labelled set**, not the KG; VLMs hallucinate (mitigated by the fixed-template visual probe, not free-form judging); range maps are often SVG (rasterised for the VLM). The non-redundancy gate is *verified* for taxa and **must be re-checked for artworks (the relational layer) before locking**. **The image axis is committed; the gate chooses the image domain, it does not abandon the modality.** Routing (post-M-BOOKS, sequenced): artwork first -> if its validity gate fails, fall back to the verified **taxa range-map** axis, which **guarantees an image axis** by construction. Taxa de-risks the modality; it does not let it be dropped.
- **Obscurity-vs-reliability tension (new, manage via sitelink band).** The content/structure gap is driven by entity obscurity: the entities where content-absence shows an effect (sparse triples, rich descriptions) are also where the KG is least reliable and the LLM has least parametric knowledge. Mitigated by sampling within a sitelink band (§5.1) — obscure enough for descriptive signal, well-formed enough for trustworthy structure — but it remains a balance to monitor during entity selection.
- **The "reasoned-supportable" category is the thorniest measurement.** Retrieved and fabricated are clean; "reasoned-supportable" (a valid multi-hop path that entails the claim) is hard to distinguish from plausibly fabricated. The entailment gate (§5.5) mitigates this — a path that does not entail the claim is *not* labelled reasoned-supportable — and §5.8 reports this category's error rate explicitly. The three-way split — our differentiator from a two-way supported/unsupported judgement — rests on the hardest-to-measure category. Budget accordingly.
- **Curated-KG validity.** With a curated KG and designed questions, results are findings on a controlled testbed, not universal laws. RQ2 is framed as controlled manipulation, not prediction, and the ablation manifest is fixed before inspection, to avoid engineering the observed effect.
- **Thin-but-extensible is still thin.** The minimal core must be fully measured on its one comparison; extensibility buys future axes, not permission to be preliminary on the first.
