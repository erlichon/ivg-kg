## 1. Problem

Large language models produce fluent, confident answers, but a reader cannot tell *how* any given statement was arrived at. Three failure-relevant cases are conflated in the output: facts the model **retrieved** from reliable structured knowledge, facts it obtained by **reasoning** over several pieces of structured knowledge, and facts it **fabricated**. (What a tool can measure post-hoc is *supportability* — whether the evidence needed to ground a claim exists in the graph — so from §3 onward the middle category is named **reasoned-supportable**.) Knowledge graphs (KGs) store high-quality factual triples about entities; *multimodal* KGs additionally attach images and textual descriptions to each entity, making them a rich substrate for multimedia analytics. Our **taxa slice** instantiates this multimodality directly — fact-bearing range-map images alongside descriptions and triples — while a text-rich **books** slice anchors the textual-content modality; the study examines each evidence modality **within its slice**, with an unconfounded cross-modality contrast drawn within the taxa slice (see §3). Yet the connection between what an LLM *says* and what the KG *contains* has not been made visually inspectable, and — more importantly — the *cause* of a grounding failure is not separable from its symptom.

This project builds an interactive visual-analytics instrument that makes grounding inspectable **and** turns grounding failure into something an analyst can manipulate and measure under controlled conditions, rather than merely observe.

## 2. Core framing and scientific position

The project's spine is a single phenomenon: **absence-induced hallucination** — fabrication that occurs when the evidence needed to ground a claim is not in front of the model. We decompose this phenomenon by the **modality of the missing evidence**:

- **Content-absence** — the entity's non-structural content is missing, even though structural triples are present. The **primary** content modality is **textual** (entity descriptions / glosses / abstracts), because text content reliably carries query-relevant facts, anchored on the **books** slice. **Images are a gated second content modality, admitted only where they demonstrably encode queryable facts**: general-purpose KG images (Wikidata P18, and book cover/identity images) are *decorative* and excluded, so the image modality is realized on a **second, image-bearing slice — biological taxa with range-map images (Wikidata P181)** — where an empirical non-redundancy check (§10) confirms the map carries spatial-distribution facts absent from both the taxon's triples (for the majority) and its description (always).
- **Knowledge-absence** — the structural fact is not in the model's context, whether because it is absent from the KG (incompleteness) or present but not retrieved (retrieval-locality). These two share an identical model-facing symptom — the fact is simply not in context — and are therefore treated as a single failure mode rather than two axes.

The central question is whether the *modality* of the missing evidence changes the model's grounding behaviour, and whether an analyst can see, manipulate, and repair this through a visual interface. This framing makes "multimedia" load-bearing rather than decorative: the experiment manipulates each **evidence modality — structural knowledge, textual content, and image content** — and measures its effect on grounding. Because the two content modalities are richest on different slices (text on books, image on taxa), the primary comparison is **within-modality, reported per slice**, anchored by **knowledge-absence (present on both slices)**; an unconfounded *cross-modality* contrast is a **second step**, drawn only within the taxa slice where one entity carries all three (see RQ2, §11).

**Critical design constraint — content/structure non-redundancy.** The comparison is only meaningful if the textual content carries facts the triples do *not* already encode. If descriptions merely paraphrase the triples, ablating content changes nothing the model cannot recover from structure, and the central comparison nulls out. The domain slice must therefore be chosen so that some questions depend on *content-only* facts (present in the description, absent from every triple). This is verified empirically before the design is locked (see §10).

### Relationship to prior work (what we adopt, extend, and differentiate from)

- **Claim-level attribution is adopted in spirit, then reimplemented.** KGR [1] decomposes a response into atomic claims and verifies them against KG triples, and VeGraph [6] represents claims as triplets and verifies them iteratively. None ships reusable **KG-grounded** code for our setting (GraphEval [4] is document/NLI-grounded with no public code; VeGraph is Wikipedia-derived), so we **reimplement** the KGR-style claim-attribution pipeline and **extend** it from a two-way (supported / unsupported) judgement to the three-way **retrieved / reasoned-supportable / fabricated** distinction.
- **The reasoning-vs-retrieval distinction is motivated by mechanism.** "Why LLMs Hallucinate on Structured Knowledge" [7] shows mechanistically that, when grounding evidence is absent from context, models revert to parametric memory. [7] studies the model's **internal states**; our tool does not measure that mechanism — it makes **claim supportability against the KG legible** per claim, which [7] motivates but does not amount to a measurement of the internal process.
- **Interactive KG repair exists; measuring which repairs matter does not.** CogMG [5] already performs query → detect-gap → complete-triple → update-KG → improve-answer with a human-in-the-loop interface. We differentiate by measuring **repair leverage** — how many downstream claims change status per repair — which CogMG does not.
- **Visual source attribution has been done for documents, not for graph structure.** VISA [2] draws bounding boxes on document screenshots to attribute answers to evidence. We are the **knowledge-graph analogue**: attribution to triples and subgraph paths rather than image regions.
- **The claim-extraction and retrofitting pipeline is inherited.** KGR [1] decomposes responses into atomic claims and verifies/retrofits them against KG triples. We turn its batch verification loop into an interactive, inspectable one and add the **reasoned-supportable** (reasoned-over-a-path) category it collapses.
- **Interaction and justification design follows the KG-visual-analytics roadmap.** Li et al. [3] find that practitioners want LLM outputs justified against the KG, with a two-panel design linking response text to verified subgraph structure — directly informing our interface.

## 3. Research questions

The original three questions are retained, each reframed to the honest, defensible register established during scoping.

**RQ1 — Where do LLMs hallucinate, and can it be made legible?**
Can claim-level attribution against the knowledge graph reliably separate **retrieved** (a direct triple supports the claim), **reasoned-supportable** (a multi-hop path supports it), and **fabricated** (no supporting evidence) statements, and can a visual interface make this distinction legible to a non-expert? *We make claim **supportability** legible — whether the evidence to ground the claim exists in the graph — not the model's internal reasoning process. Novelty lies in the visual legibility and the three-way split, not in the attribution algorithm.*

**RQ2 — Does the absence of evidence change hallucination, and does it depend on the evidence's modality?**
*Phase A (minimal, done first).* For each evidence modality independently — **textual content** (description, books slice), **image content** (range-map image, taxa slice), and **structural knowledge** (triples, both slices) — does withholding that evidence from the model's context measurably raise the fabrication rate on queries about that entity? Effects are reported **per slice**; because content modality is confounded with domain across slices, no head-to-head text-vs-image contrast is claimed here, and **knowledge-absence — run identically on both slices — serves as a shared reference manipulation (establishing that each slice responds to absence) rather than a cross-modality comparand**. The image-content axis is conditional on its validity gate (non-redundancy + visual-probe error, §10).
*Phase B (second step, time-permitting).* On the **taxa slice**, where one entity carries image, description, and triples, run all three ablations on the **same entities** for an *unconfounded* cross-modality contrast — with the caveat that taxa descriptions are generic, so a weak text effect there reflects content-poverty, not modality. **If time is short, Phase B is curtailed first, with no loss to the Phase-A core.** *Framed as controlled manipulation, not prediction, to avoid engineering a correlation.*

**RQ3 — Which repairs matter? (repair leverage)**
When an analyst adds a missing triple or restores missing content and the answer is re-grounded, *how many* downstream claims change status per repair — i.e., are some gaps high-leverage (resolving a cascade of dependent claims) while others are leaves? *Differentiates from CogMG via the leverage metric.*

## 4. Intended contributions

1. An **interactive visual-analytics instrument** that attributes each LLM claim to its supporting KG structure and classifies it as retrieved, reasoned-supportable, or fabricated, made legible through coordinated views.
2. A **controlled decomposition of absence-induced hallucination by evidence modality** — measuring **per slice** whether withholding textual content, image content, or structural knowledge changes grounding behaviour on curated KG slices (a text-rich books slice and a multimodal taxa slice), with an **unconfounded within-taxa cross-modality contrast as a second step** where one entity carries all three modalities.
3. A **repair-leverage measure** that quantifies which missing pieces of the KG, when restored interactively, resolve the most downstream fabrication.

## 5. Method (pointwise)

1. **KG construction.** Take a bounded domain slice of **live Wikidata** (entities + triples + textual descriptions; general-purpose P18 images excluded as decorative — a *dedicated* fact-bearing image axis is added on a second slice, below), frozen as a local graph with full ground-truth control. Live Wikidata is chosen over frozen scraped-image Freebase sets (MMKG/FB15k-IMG) because it avoids link rot, is enrichable, has reliably fact-bearing descriptions, and matches the structural lineage of KGR and CogMG. The domain slice is **books (Wikidata `Q571`)**, selected empirically via the content/structure-overlap check (§10): book descriptions frequently carry content-only facts the triples omit (genre, form, tradition/affiliation, scope, descriptive role — e.g. "key text in the Kagyu tradition of Tibetan Buddhism," "53-volume Greek anthology"), whereas mature domains such as chemical compounds showed the description merely paraphrasing the triples. Entities are sampled within a **sitelink band** (roughly 5–40 sitelinks): obscure enough that descriptions still add information, well-formed enough that the structure layer is trustworthy — avoiding both the auto-imported junk tail and the over-documented famous works whose triples subsume their descriptions. Produce a **KG-full** (ground truth) and an **ablation manifest** specifying, per experiment, which content fields (descriptions) and which triples are removed. The manifest is fixed before results are inspected. A **second slice — biological taxa with range-map images (P181)** — supplies the image-content axis (§2, §7). It is sampled in the same sitelink band and **filtered to taxa whose range is not already a structured triple**, so the map is non-redundant by construction (the taxa analogue of the books band). The pipeline distinguishes two sets. **Evidence** is what the generator may see — triples, description, and range-map image — and is what a perturbation withholds. The **grading reference** is what the classifier always grades against: KG-full triples plus a small curated set of content-only labels (the content-only textual facts for books, the hand-labelled map facts for taxa). The image is *evidence*, not a label, so image claims are graded against the curated label rather than the raster. The live pull falls back to the independent **QLever** endpoint when WDQS is degraded.
2. **Question bank.** A fixed set of questions spanning one-hop (retrieval), multi-hop (reasoning), and questions targeting ablated entities, with difficulty tiers mirroring KGR's Simple/complex/multi-hop structure. For the content-absence axis specifically, questions target the content-only fact types that book descriptions reliably carry but triples omit — genre/form ("is this a play or a novel?"), tradition/affiliation ("what tradition is this text central to?"), scope/structure ("how many volumes?"), and descriptive role. This ensures the content-absence axis can produce signal rather than nulling out.
3. **Answer generation.** The model answers **in-context**: the (possibly ablated) evidence for the target entity — triples, description, and range-map image where in scope — is assembled into the prompt, and the LLM/VLM produces a chain-of-thought draft from that context (precomputed and stored for demo safety). A perturbation removes evidence from this **generation context** (it does not alter the grading reference); the image axis uses a VLM.
4. **Claim extraction.** KGR-style decomposition into atomic claims.
5. **Grounding & classification.** Per claim: entity-link to KG nodes; a **single piece of reference evidence that entails the claim — a direct triple *or* a content fact (description / curated image-fact label)** → *retrieved* (the modality of that evidence is recorded); else a multi-hop path (up to *k* hops) that **entails** the claim → *reasoned-supportable*; else *fabricated*. Entailment is **value-sensitive** — a claim asserting a value the evidence contradicts or omits fails the gate and is *fabricated*, so a merely-existing triple/path is **not** mislabeled — and is checked by an NLI gate against the serialized reference evidence. Image-content claims are graded the same way against their **curated label, not the raster** (the fixed-template visual probe is used to construct/verify those labels, not to grade claims). The attribution **reimplements the KGR-style pipeline** (§2) extended from two-way to this three-way split, and grades against the **grading reference**, never the ablated context.
6. **Coverage & ablation.** Per entity, record content completeness (description present; image present where applicable) and structural degree. Under the manifest, ablate textual content, image content, or structural evidence independently and measure the shift in claim-status distribution. Classification always grades against the full **grading reference** (§5.1), never the ablated context, so a fact hidden from the generator remains gradable; **knowledge-absence is realized as withholding triples from the generation context** (covering both incompleteness and retrieval-locality, §2) while those triples remain in the grading reference.
7. **Repair loop (live).** Analyst restores a missing triple/content; answer is regenerated; claims 4–5 re-run; status diffed before/after; **repair leverage** logged.
8. **Measurement-error accounting.** The grounding classifier's own error rate is quantified on a held-out hand-labelled sample, **reported per modality path — the textual NLI gate and the visual probe separately** (the visual path is expected to be noisier) — so all downstream numbers are interpretable. Because the image axis is graded against a curated hand-labelled reference rather than the KG, those map labels are **double-labelled / spot-checked** so the reference is not a single unverified judgement.

## 6. Interface (three coordinated panels)

- **Answer panel** — the LLM response with each claim colour-coded by status (retrieved / reasoned-supportable / fabricated); clicking a claim highlights its support in the graph.
- **Subgraph panel** — the local multimodal subgraph (entity images/text), with direct-support triples and multi-hop supporting paths highlighted in matching colours.
- **Analytics panel** — modality-coverage statistics per entity, claim-status distributions, and repair history (fabrication count before/after each repair, with leverage).

The defining interaction is the **controlled repair loop**: spot a fabricated claim → inspect the gap → restore the missing evidence → regenerate → watch the claim's status change and the analytics update. This realises the sensemaking cycle (Pirolli–Card) with each repair as one iteration; in Brehmer–Munzner terms the analyst's task is *discover* (where grounding fails) and *produce* (repair), with the subgraph as the *how*.

## 7. Extensible-by-design architecture

The minimal core instruments **one comparison** — content-absence vs. knowledge-absence — fully and rigorously. Extensibility is bought with exactly three interfaces, not a framework:

- **Perturbation-as-interface.** The pipeline consumes a KG plus a *perturbation* object. The minimal core fully instruments **text-content-absence vs. knowledge-absence** on the books slice; the same seam additionally carries an **image-content-absence** axis on the taxa slice, **gated** — reported as a result only if its per-domain non-redundancy check and visual-probe error accounting hold (§10). Further axes (entity-linking, conflict/staleness) are new perturbation objects added without rewriting the pipeline.
- **Uniform per-claim structured log.** Every claim is logged as (claim, linked entities, grounding path, status, active perturbation), plus the classifier's error rate. Every future axis writes into the same log, so analytics and any later factorial analysis come almost free.
- **Control-generated-from-available-perturbations UI.** The interface renders perturbation controls from the registered perturbation types, so adding an axis adds a control automatically.

**Deferred and named (not built):** the multi-axis factorial; additional failure axes (entity-linking, conflict/staleness); and — stated as an explicit scope boundary — **live diagnosis**. The tool supports *controlled* manipulation (analyst ablates a known entity and observes the effect); it does **not** claim to identify the cause of an arbitrary unseen hallucination, because content-absence and knowledge-absence are output-indistinguishable without the ground-truth of the manipulation that only exists in the controlled setting.

## 8. Evaluation design

A dual approach, per the course's evaluation lecture:

- **Analytic / simulated (primary evidence).** Quantitative results from the fixed question bank — claim-status distributions under content-ablation vs. knowledge-absence, before/after repair fabrication counts, and the repair-leverage distribution. The image-content-ablation axis is reported as a **gated** result — validity-conditional on its non-redundancy check and visual-probe error accounting (§5.8, §10). Runs without recruited users.
- **Qualitative (illustrative).** 2–3 end-to-end walkthrough case studies showing a fabrication caught and repaired, in the spirit of KGR's multi-hop case study; an informal think-aloud with a few peers if time permits.

A full user study is stated as future work — standard and defensible for a VIS short-paper scope. The classifier-error accounting (§5.8) is reported so the analytic results are trustworthy.

## 9. Implementation

Plotly/Dash, built on the course starter repository; code at https://github.com/erlichon/ivg-kg. Three coordinated `dcc.Graph`/custom components sharing a `dcc.Store`; a precomputed store holding answers + claims + groundings keyed by question (demo-safe, instant); NetworkX backing the subgraph and path search; one live callback for repair-regeneration (a single cheap LLM/VLM call on a small context); the image axis uses a local open VLM for image-grounded generation, and a fixed-template binary visual probe (not free-form VLM judging) to construct/verify the curated image labels that image-content claims are then graded against, with the specific model and probe detailed in the implementation spec. The subgraph component (path highlighting + node content) is the part most likely to require going beyond off-the-shelf components, which the demo rubric rewards.

**Subgraph rendering — open sub-decision.** Evaluate Dash-native options (Dash Cytoscape, or Plotly network rendering) against a heavier JS SDK (e.g. yFiles, which is purpose-built for interactive KG visualisation but is commercial and non-trivial to embed in Dash). Default to the Dash-native path unless its path-highlighting / dynamic-layout limits prove blocking; the JS SDK is a fallback, not a starting point, given the Plotly/Dash course requirement.

## 10. Scope, risks, and honest boundaries

- **Data dependency (resolved).** The substrate is **live Wikidata**: a **books (`Q571`)** slice for text-content vs. knowledge, and a **taxa** slice with range-map images (`P181`) for the gated image-content axis. General-purpose P18 images are dropped as decorative, but range maps are a *dedicated fact-bearing* image type (verified by the non-redundancy gate below); no off-the-shelf MMKG suits QA, as the existing ones target entity alignment / link prediction. The content/structure-overlap check confirmed books carry content-only facts the triples omit, where mature domains (e.g. chemical compounds) did not. The live pull falls back to the independent **QLever** endpoint when WDQS is degraded. If a slice proves insufficient at scale, the fallback is another sparsely-structured Wikidata domain or knowledge-absence alone.
- **Image-axis cost and validity (gated).** Genuine image non-redundancy means the fact is *not* in the triples, so image claims are graded against a **small hand-labelled set**, not the KG; VLMs hallucinate (mitigated by the fixed-template visual probe, not free-form judging); range maps are often SVG (rasterised for the VLM). The non-redundancy gate passed for taxa but **must be re-checked per domain before locking**. **Fallback:** if the image path underperforms its gate, both the image-content result (Phase A) and the within-taxa cross-modality contrast (Phase B) are dropped, leaving the fully-measured **text-content vs. knowledge-absence** spine on books with no loss to the Phase-A core.
- **Obscurity-vs-reliability tension (new, manage via sitelink band).** The content/structure gap is driven by entity obscurity: the entities where content-absence shows an effect (sparse triples, rich descriptions) are also where the KG is least reliable and the LLM has least parametric knowledge. Mitigated by sampling within a sitelink band (§5.1) — obscure enough for descriptive signal, well-formed enough for trustworthy structure — but it remains a balance to monitor during entity selection.
- **The "reasoned-supportable" category is the thorniest measurement.** Retrieved and fabricated are clean; "reasoned-supportable" (a valid multi-hop path that entails the claim) is hard to distinguish from plausibly fabricated. The entailment gate (§5.5) mitigates this — a path that does not entail the claim is *not* labelled reasoned-supportable — and §5.8 reports this category's error rate explicitly. The three-way split — our differentiator from a two-way supported/unsupported judgement — rests on the hardest-to-measure category. Budget accordingly.
- **Curated-KG validity.** With a curated KG and designed questions, results are findings on a controlled testbed, not universal laws. RQ2 is framed as controlled manipulation, not prediction, and the ablation manifest is fixed before inspection, to avoid engineering the observed effect.
- **Thin-but-extensible is still thin.** The minimal core must be fully measured on its one comparison; extensibility buys future axes, not permission to be preliminary on the first.
